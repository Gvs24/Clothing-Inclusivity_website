Allergic to Certain Fabrics?

Fabric Choices for Sensitive Skin

Some fabrics may cause irritation or allergic reactions. Here are some fabric alternatives and tips to consider.

Common Fabrics That May Cause Allergies

  • Polyester: A synthetic fabric often used in inexpensive clothing. May cause skin irritation for sensitive individuals.
  • Wool: Natural fiber, but can be itchy or cause allergic reactions in some people.
  • Latex: Common in elastic waistbands or sportswear. Known for causing rashes or reactions in sensitive people.

Fabric Alternatives

  • Organic Cotton: Soft and breathable, often a better choice for sensitive skin. Pricing: Cheap to Moderate.
  • Bamboo Fabric: A soft, breathable alternative to synthetic fibers, and naturally hypoallergenic. Pricing: Moderate to Pricey.
  • Linen: A natural fiber that’s cool and gentle on the skin, great for hot weather. Pricing: Moderate.
  • Silk: Luxurious and smooth, typically better for sensitive skin. Pricing: Pricey.
  • Hemp: Eco-friendly, strong, and breathable, with natural hypoallergenic properties. Pricing: Moderate.
